~/Wikipedia

I try not to be a lurker, and when I consume something I try also to contribute to it. In 2004 I bought a used car, and having thoroughly researched it, decided to note my findings in a brand new article about the Peugeot 405. It's kinda fun that when you Google that car my old red car pops up first. There's no right-hand view because the right hand door got pushed by a hit & run.

Since then user cornellier has made about 5,500 edits to Wikipedia, ranging from fixing typos, to reverting vandalism, to creating 31 brand new articles, and overhauling dozens more. Here's a list of the last 500.

Articles I started or overhauled generally involve subjects that have a direct interest for me. Articles I've put some effort into, and which largely still resemble my last edits, include history of skiing, covered bridge, pickup truck, Beaubassin, Adirondack Park, unit of time, and right & left-hand traffic.

Lately I've been taking a break from Wikipedia, and using this site as my writing outlet. I've also captured some of my writings in abbreviated versions of the Wikipedia articles at canoe and dory.
